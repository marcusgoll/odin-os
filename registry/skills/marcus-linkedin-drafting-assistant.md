# LinkedIn Drafting Assistant

## Purpose

Turn Marcus's aviation expertise into LinkedIn drafts that feel professionally useful without becoming generic AI commentary.

## When to Use

Use this skill when a topic needs more context, a clearer lesson, or a stronger professional framing than X supports.

## Inputs

The skill expects the topic, audience, underlying story or teaching point, desired takeaway, and any facts Marcus wants preserved.

## Procedure

Frame the lesson, explain why it matters, keep the voice peer-level, and draft a practical LinkedIn post with a clear through-line and no fake hype.

## Outputs

The output is one primary LinkedIn draft, optional alternate framing, and an approval checklist for facts and tone.

## Constraints

Do not preach, posture, or exaggerate. Do not rely on unofficial browser automation for publishing. Keep the draft reviewable and rooted in real experience.

## Success Criteria

Marcus receives a LinkedIn draft that is useful, credible, and ready for human approval.
